THE LEONARDO 3.4.5 MODEL

THE ORGANIZATION AND ITS STRATEGIC OPTIONS

The concept of a vertical hierarchy of complexity has been developed by J.G. Miller (Living Systems) together with the concept of horizontal specializations (J.G. Miller’s 20 subsystems). Taking these concepts from Miller, and other precise empirical research on his work, the integration of C.G. Jung’s psychological typology with an organizational concept based on life sciences plus an empirical observation on how preferences at work influence decision making in strategy, have led us to be able to create this unique tool.

THE DIFFERENT PROFILES

Leonardo 3.4.5 profiles are based on 3 “active ingredients”:

HIGHLY ACCURATE PROFILES

More than 1500 different profiles are available in each of the following languages: English, French, Dutch, German and Italian. They describe preferred behaviours and provide guidance for teamwork and the development of collective intelligence. “Devilishly precise” as said several participants in workshops, but this precision is necessary in order to gain the trust from the participants and to give credibility to the guidance which is offered in the profiles. Of course, a profiles generator, regardless of how precise it is, is only a trivial machine, and it will never be able to describe the complexity of human beings. But approximately 90% of the participants are able to bridge the gap between their professional life history and what they find in their profiles without further questions. In 10% of cases some precision is requested or the need for more personal guidance emerges. This is why we have our consultants on hand, who have mastered this tool, thus providing support for navigation and interpretation of the profiles.

LEVERAGING NEURO-SCIENCE IN LEARNING

The profiles are equipped with “learning and change facilitators” inspired by Frederick Vester’s approach of “Learning Biology”. The profiles take into account the “mechanics” of memorisation and the time needed to complete a process for building awareness and for making desired change options available for daily use.

BRAIN-FRIENDLY WRITING STYLE

This has contributed significantly to the writing technique so that the mere reading of the text is an individual facilitation and coaching session by itself. Easy-reading tools like reframing, change of life history, etc. are deployed to ease participants learning and change processes. One time reading might be enough for some participants whereas for others a thorough work on the development profile and attentive reading several times will achieve the desired effect. This sophisticated architecture of the profiles, rewards consultants regularly with comments of relief from participants like: “First I don’t have a big deal to change in my behaviour”, and second, “it’s easy to achieve; everything is clear and O.K. for me!” Several messages of this kind indicate that the “Instant Behavioural Optimization” is operating its magic and that the remaining time of the workshop can be devoted to pragmatic solution finding as well as to contracting common objectives and individual contributions. If few members of a larger group do not demonstrate any shift towards this state, the group dynamics exert a powerful training so that they can easily come around with a few individual coaching sessions.
